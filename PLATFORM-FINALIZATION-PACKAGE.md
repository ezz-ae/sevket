# Ölmez / AFFAREM Platform Finalization Package

## 1. Executive critique

The live platform had a strong visual system and enough page depth to feel substantial, but several public lines still read like internal draft logic. The largest trust breaks were operational words used as investor-facing status labels, repeated "deployment" language on opportunity surfaces, old contact domains, a People page that stopped at 48 roles, article pages without engagement signals, and one leadership line that publicly explained who had been excluded from an edit.

The upgrade direction is now: Ölmez as the master brand, AFFAREM as the investor and operating-control layer, Shawerma Time as the gas-station restaurant opportunity brand, and Sevet Ölmez as the founder-facing public voice. The platform should read as a controlled restaurant business company, not a restaurant brochure.

## 2. Page-by-page issues and replacements

Home:
Use the core line sparingly: "We are not investing in a restaurant company. We are building a restaurant business company." Replace public "Capital deployment" language with "Investment access" and "Tracked capital." Keep the founder image in the founder/article layer, not as repeated decoration.

Founder:
Replace personality-brand framing with founder-as-operating-voice framing. Use `/brand-library/olmez-founder-current.png`. Founder copy should be direct, sober, and operational.

Leadership:
Remove all exclusion language. Replacement positioning: "A focused leadership series explaining the people who carry culture, management, government relations, finance, events, sales, and governance inside the company."

People:
Replace repeated role-count language with country and department hiring structure. Use `/brand-library/olmez-people-growth.png` and the founder-style people line: "From 5 people to nearly 3,000 people, the system stayed the same: discipline first, opportunity second, growth third."

Opportunities:
Public statuses should read as `Available`, `Under Review`, `Reserved`, `Operational`, `Opening soon`, or `Accepting Junior Investors`. "Deployment" belongs only in internal execution context.

Investors:
Add responsibility, wallet, dashboard, risk, KYC, tax, support, message, payout, and statement modules. Replace passive-return copy with controlled reporting and eligible distribution language.

Contact:
Replace generic routing with department cards and correct `olmez.us` email addresses.

Social Responsibility:
Add Global Ölmez Funding Department. Make it infrastructure responsibility, not charity marketing.

Magazine / Articles:
Add article views, likes, read time, dates, author, category, and people-reading-now signals. Add founder essays and use the specified founder image.

Stories:
Create human success stories with believable engagement numbers and page-level live count.

## 6. Compliance-safe investment wording

Use:
`projected`, `estimated`, `eligible distribution`, `based on actual performance`, `after reconciliation`, `subject to operating costs`, `subject to reserve rules`, `qualified investors only`, `requires review`.

Required distribution line:
`Eligible net distributions are reviewed and processed twice weekly after sales reconciliation, operating costs, reserves, fees, and applicable deductions.`

Avoid:
`guaranteed ROI`, `risk-free`, `fixed return`, `passive income guaranteed`, `automatic profit`, `secure profit`, `no risk`, `guaranteed payout`.

## 7. Investor Responsibility page content

Core line:
`An investor does not only enter the return side of the business. They enter the responsibility side first.`

Sections:
starter investor responsibilities, junior investor responsibilities, branch investor responsibilities, four-investor model, operator responsibility, manager-hired model, reporting expectations, risk understanding, AFFAREM account responsibility, distribution expectations, communication standards, discipline score, and what investors should not expect.

## 8. Global Ölmez Funding Department content

From October 2026, Ölmez launches a department funded through `0.5% of eligible investor distributions`. Purpose: high-risk discovery, early market education, infrastructure research, community-backed business development, and expansion-readiness work in Saudi Arabia, Greece, and Jordan.

Projected target:
`Total projected investment support: $50M by end of 2028.`

Required line:
`The purpose is not to donate randomly. The purpose is to build responsible entry points before expansion becomes aggressive.`

## 9. Junior Investor Program content

Core message:
`Junior Investment Pools let new investors learn the Ölmez system through a controlled restaurant pool before entering a specific unit.`

Rules:
Pool from `$1,000` to `$12,000`, max package `$12,000`, six-month subscription, no penalty for early closure, exit after at least two distributions, early exit may reduce future priority, strongly recommended for limited managerial or food-business experience.

Calculator:
Budget slider from `$1,000` to `$12,000` with estimated eligible distribution ranges. Projections are not guaranteed.
